Fades and Tapers

The fade is good choice for guys that enjoy shorter hair. It offers a very clean, low maintenance style often worn by military personel. A taper is similar, only showing up in the temple/sideburn area and the nape. If you like this style it is important to find a barber that does well. A fade without a smooth gradient is worse than not getting the haircut.

The Classics

For those that appreciate a more conservative look, the classics are perfect! The side part, quiff, pompadore, textured fringe and many others require more maintenance and styling but the end results speak for themselves and will always be great hairstyles.

Mullets

Popular in the 1980's, the mullet has come back with a modern spin on the style. Leaving the top and back longer with short sides, it is ideal for guys with curly hair. Emphasizing texture, it's important to make sure it is long enough for the mullet effect but not so long that it falls flat.

Flows

Great for guys with straight or wavy hair, the flow is a popular, mid-length hairstyle adding some texture and messiness to your overall look. It's a fairly low maintenance hairstlye that frames your face and softens facial featuers.
